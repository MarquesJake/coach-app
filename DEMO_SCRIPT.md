# Coach Intelligence Demo Script

## Full 3 to 5 Minute Script

### Opening

"Coach Intelligence helps clubs turn coach search from guesswork and network noise into a clear, evidence-led decision."

[Click: Dashboard]

"This is the command centre. It shows the live mandates we are working on, the decisions that need attention, and the intelligence around coaches, clubs and agents."

### QPR Core Demo

[Click: Mandates -> QPR Head Coach Succession]

"I will start with QPR because this is the clearest example of the product."

"The problem here is succession planning. QPR have a credible current coach profile, but they need to prepare for external interest or instability. The brief is not just find a coach. It is more specific than that."

"They need someone who can develop young players, improve player value, connect the academy pathway, and still work within Championship constraints."

[Click: Workspace]

"This workspace takes the club context, mandate requirements, evidence layer, and decision layer, and puts them in one place."

[Point to Suggested Longlist]

"This first section is the system discovery layer. It surfaces coaches with signals that match the brief. In this case, the system is looking for youth development, academy pathway, and player growth evidence."

"This is not the final answer. It is the starting point. It helps us find profiles that might otherwise be missed, but every name still needs football judgement."

[Point to Curated Longlist]

"This is where expert review comes in. The curated longlist is the human refinement layer. We are asking whether the profile makes sense for QPR, not just statistically, but realistically."

"So you can see different types of candidates: a primary target, viable option, stretch option, benchmark profile, development specialist, and European development coach."

"The labels matter because a board does not just need names. They need to understand what kind of option each coach represents."

[Point to Shortlist]

"Then we move into the shortlist. This is the decision layer. The shortlist is smaller, more realistic, and more board-ready."

"For QPR, Brian Barry-Murphy is positioned as the primary target because the development profile is strong. Will Still is a viable option with a modern game model. Liam Rosenior is a stretch option. Julien Stephan is used as a benchmark because he represents the current profile QPR are planning around."

[Point to Board Recommendation]

"And this is the output. The board recommendation does not blindly pick the highest score. It explains the recommendation in football language."

"It says why the candidate fits, what the main risk is, and what the alternative option looks like."

"For QPR, the recommendation favours long-term development upside over immediate Championship certainty. That is the key trade-off. It is not pretending there is no risk. It is making the risk explicit."

[Optional click: Brian Barry-Murphy Coach Profile]

"If I open the coach dossier, we can go deeper into the profile: current role, development model, tactical identity, career timeline, confidence, and where this coach is linked to active mandates."

"This is important because the product is not just a list of coaches. It creates a decision trail."

### Brighton Contrast

[Click: Mandates -> Brighton Head Coach Succession]

"Now Brighton is a different problem."

"This is a Premier League club with a strong identity and a clear player trading model. The issue is not simply replacing a coach. The issue is protecting the football model."

[Click: Workspace]

"The mandate here is about maintaining identity, progressive football, and player value creation."

"So the recommendation language changes. The platform is not using the same logic for every club."

"For Brighton, Francesco Farioli is the primary target profile. Kieran McKenna is a viable option, but harder to execute. Liam Rosenior is a stretch profile. Fabian Huerzeler is the benchmark profile because he represents the current Brighton identity."

[Point to Board Recommendation]

"The board recommendation here favours identity continuity and player trading upside over low-risk Premier League familiarity."

"That is a very different decision from QPR. Brighton can take more risk if the coach protects the model and helps the club keep developing and trading players."

### Bolton Practical Case

[Click: Mandates -> Bolton Head Coach Succession]

"Bolton is different again."

"This is a League One promotion case. The need is more practical: stability, efficiency, and promotion reliability."

[Click: Workspace]

"So the shortlist is more pragmatic. Michael Duff is the primary target profile because he fits the promotion and stability brief. Ryan Lowe is a viable option. John Mousinho is a stretch option. Steven Schumacher is the benchmark profile."

[Point to Board Recommendation]

"The recommendation here favours promotion reliability and efficiency over high-upside tactical experimentation."

"That is the point of showing three clubs. The same platform adapts to different football problems: Brighton need identity and trading continuity, QPR need development and sustainability, Bolton need promotion reliability."

### Close

"So the product is not an automated coach picker."

"It is data-assisted decision support for football leadership."

"It helps a club define the problem, discover evidence-backed options, apply expert judgement, and reach a board-ready recommendation."

"The value is not replacing football judgement. The value is making that judgement clearer, faster, and easier to defend."

## Key Talking Points

- One platform, three different football problems.
- QPR shows the full process: system discovery, expert curation, shortlist, recommendation.
- Brighton proves the product can handle Premier League identity and player trading decisions.
- Bolton proves it also works for practical lower-league promotion decisions.
- Suggested Longlist is discovery, not the final answer.
- Curated Longlist is human refinement.
- Shortlist is the decision layer.
- Board Recommendation explains the trade-off in football language.
- Core message: data assisted decision support, not automated recruitment.
